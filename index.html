Urban Wealth Through Morning Transit

This analysis explores the correlation between morning public transit usage (7 AM) and urban wealth metrics across major global cities. The data reveals a fascinating inverse relationship: cities with the highest GDP per capita often show moderate morning transit usage, challenging conventional assumptions about public transportation as merely an economic necessity.

Key insight: The most economically prosperous cities demonstrate a "transit sweet spot" - neither too high (indicating sole dependence on public options) nor too low (indicating poor infrastructure). This pattern suggests optimal urban wealth distribution occurs when citizens have transportation choice flexibility rather than necessity-driven dependence.
