Books I Recommend

  • Thinking, Fast and Slow – Daniel Kahneman
    Kahneman breaks down how we think in two systems: fast (intuitive) and slow (deliberate). Helped me reflect on biases and cognitive friction.
  • Predictably Irrational – Dan Ariely
    Demonstrates how humans make irrational yet predictable decisions. Pushed me to see strategy and pricing through a behavioral lens.
  • The Almanack of Naval Ravikant – Eric Jorgenson
    Naval’s ideas on wealth, leverage, and clarity made this a handbook I revisit often.
  • Nudge – Richard Thaler & Cass Sunstein
    Explains how small environmental tweaks can nudge people toward better decisions — a key tool for product and public policy.
