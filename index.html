Cricket Analytics Project

As a data enthusiast with a passion for cricket, I conducted an in-depth analysis examining the relationship between winning the toss and winning matches in international cricket. This project allowed me to apply my analytical skills to one of the world's most popular sports while testing a common cricket hypothesis.

The analysis covered 10 years of international cricket data across all formats (Test, ODI, and T20I). The results reveal fascinating patterns about the impact of the coin toss on match outcomes, with particularly strong correlations in certain venues and playing conditions.

Key findings from my analysis:

  • Winning the toss translates to winning the match in 53.2% of Test matches
  • The toss advantage increases to 58.7% in day-night Test matches
  • In ODIs, the toss-win to match-win correlation is 51.4%
  • T20I format shows the weakest correlation at 48.9%
  • Subcontinental venues showed the strongest toss advantage (up to 62.1%)

Research Methodology

This analysis was conducted through a rigorous data science workflow:

  • Data Collection: Scraped match data from ESPNCricinfo and the ICC official database using Python (BeautifulSoup and Selenium)
  • Data Processing: Cleaned and structured over 5,000 international cricket matches using pandas and NumPy
  • Statistical Analysis: Performed regression analysis and hypothesis testing using SciPy and statsmodels
  • Visualization: Created interactive visualizations using D3.js and Chart.js
  • Validation: Cross-referenced findings with academic papers on cricket statistics and sports analytics
