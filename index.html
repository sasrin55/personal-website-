Books I Recommend

  • Thinking, Fast and Slow – Daniel Kahneman
    Kahneman, a Nobel laureate, breaks down how we think in two systems: fast (intuitive) and slow (deliberate). I recommend it because it made me reflect on my biases and shaped how I approach problem-solving in business.
  • Predictably Irrational – Dan Ariely
    Ariely explains how humans make irrational decisions in systematic and predictable ways. I loved how it merged psychology with economics, and it pushed me to understand how to influence decision-making in strategic work.
  • The Almanack of Naval Ravikant – Eric Jorgenson (compiled from Naval's thoughts)
    This isn’t a traditional book — it’s a collection of Naval’s ideas on wealth, happiness, and leverage. I go back to it regularly because it feels timeless and highly applicable to both career and life decisions.
  • Nudge – Richard H. Thaler & Cass R. Sunstein
    Thaler and Sunstein give real-life examples of how to steer people toward better decisions without forcing them. I recommend it because it connects behavioral science with public policy and product design in a way that’s easy to apply.

Podcasts I Like

  • Naval – Naval Ravikant
    Naval's podcast is short, dense, and packed with wisdom. I listen to it for perspective on how to think independently, build leverage, and prioritize peace over busy-ness.
  • Freakonomics Radio – Hosted by Stephen Dubner
    This show dives into the unexpected side of everyday topics using data. I recommend it because it shows how economic thinking can explain (and sometimes debunk) how the world works.
  • The Knowledge Project – Hosted by Shane Parrish
    A long-form podcast featuring deep thinkers and decision-makers. It helps me refine how I think and exposes me to mental models that improve personal and professional clarity.
